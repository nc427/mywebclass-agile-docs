"GDPR Compliance for Customer Data Protection"

Description: 
    This epic aims to ensure that our company complies with the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) 
    to protect the privacy and personal data of our customers.

GDPR requirements and how to address them: 

1. Consent Management: GDPR requires organizations to obtain explicit consent from individuals before collecting and processing their personal data. 
- You can address this requirement by implementing a consent management system on your website or application that clearly explains what data you collect, how you use it, and obtaining consent from users before collecting their data.

2. Data Subject Rights: GDPR provides individuals with several rights, such as the right to access, rectify, and erase their personal data. 
- To comply with this requirement, you can implement a system that allows individuals to access their personal data, request changes, and request the deletion of their data.

3. Data Breach Notification: GDPR requires organizations to notify the appropriate authorities and individuals of any data breaches that occur. 
- To comply with this requirement, you can implement a process to detect and report data breaches quickly and efficiently, as well as have a plan in place to notify affected individuals and authorities.

4. Data Protection by Design and Default: GDPR requires organizations to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data protection by design and default.
- To comply with this requirement, you can implement data protection policies and procedures at every stage of the data lifecycle, including data collection, processing, storage, and deletion.

5. Data Processing Agreements: GDPR requires organizations to have written agreements in place with third-party processors that outline the responsibilities of each party and ensure that data is being processed in compliance with GDPR. 
- To comply with this requirement, you can implement a process to review and sign data processing agreements with third-party processors.

6. Data Protection Officer (DPO): GDPR requires organizations to appoint a DPO if they process or store large amounts of personal data, carry out large-scale monitoring of individuals, or process special categories of personal data.

Goals: 
    Ensure that all customer data is collected and processed in compliance with GDPR regulations.
    Provide transparency to customers on how their personal data is collected and processed.
    Ensure the security and integrity of customer data by implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures.
    Create a culture of data protection within the organization by training employees on GDPR compliance.

User Stories: 
    As a customer, I want to be informed about how my personal data is being collected and processed, so that I can make informed decisions about providing my data.
    As a customer, I want to have control over my personal data, including the ability to access, correct, or delete it.
    As an employee, I want to receive training on GDPR compliance, so that I can ensure that customer data is being handled appropriately.
    As an IT administrator, I want to implement technical measures to ensure the security and integrity of customer data, such as data encryption and access controls.

Dependencies: 
    Compliance with other data protection regulations may impact the implementation of GDPR compliance measures.
    Integration with existing systems and processes may be required.

Risks: 
    Non-compliance with GDPR regulations may result in legal penalties and damage to the company's reputation.
    Failure to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures may result in data breaches and loss of customer trust.

Estimated Effort: 
    6-12 months, depending on the complexity of the organization's data processing activities and existing level of compliance.

Business Value: 
    Ensuring GDPR compliance will increase customer trust and loyalty, and protect the company from legal and reputational risks.

Priority: 
    High priority, as compliance with GDPR regulations is essential for protecting customer privacy and data, and avoiding legal and reputational risks.
